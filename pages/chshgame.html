Play the CHSH game

Alice and Bob are asked bits x and y, respectively, and are asked to respond with bits a and b satisfying

a ⊕ b = xy

If each player's output is a classical function of their input with no communication, they can only win with probability 0.75. However, if the players share quantum entanglement, they can win with probability cos2(π/8) ≈ 0.8535 without communicating.

Control the players' measurements, visualised on a cross-section of the Bloch sphere, on a maximally entangled state of a single qubit and see how close you can get to the optimal winning probability.
