Research Interests

Nonlocal games: In a nonlocal game, two players (usually called Alice and Bob) are asked questions and must produce correct answers without communicating. If the players share quantum entanglement, they can often win with probability higher than for any classical strategy. This provide a powerful tool to study entanglement. Try it yourself with the classic example of the CHSH game!
A surprising property of nonlocal games is that approximating the quantum value of a general game is an undecidable computational problem; in contrast deciding the classical value is in NP. I am interested in studying how and when this undecidability translates to well-structured classes of nonlocal games, such as those coming from combinatorial problems.
