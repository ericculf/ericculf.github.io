Research Interests

Nonlocal games: In a nonlocal game, two players (usually called Alice and Bob) are asked questions and must produce correct answers without communicating. If the players share quantum entanglement, they can often win with probability higher than for any classical strategy. This provide a powerful tool to study entanglement. You can try playing the classic example of the CHSH game here!
A surprising property of nonlocal games is that approximating the quantum value of a general game is an undecidable computational problem; in contrast deciding the classical value is in NP. I am interested in studying when and how this undecidability translates to well-structured classes of nonlocal games, such as those coming from combinatorial problems.
See for example arXiv:2410.21223, arxiv:2312.16765, arxiv:2607.06876

Quantum spaces and symmetries: Any (compact topological) space can be studied through its algebra of functions. Treating a noncommutative algebra as if it were an algebra of functions gives rise to the notion of a quantum space. These naturally capture spaces of observables arising in quantum mechanics. For certain nonlocal games, the perfect strategies can be described using a quantum space, corresponding the quantum symmetries of an underlying combinatorial object. I am interested in understanding the consequences of these quantum symmetries, as well as other places where quantum spaces arise in quantum information science.
See for example arxiv:2509.07835, arXiv:2309.12887, arxiv:2502.01974

Quantum cryptography: Quantum states satisfy the no-cloning property, which states that no physical process can copy an unknown quantum state. This enables the development of classically-impossible cryptoraphy, the most famous of which is quantum key distribution (QKD), where two parties can generate a secret key over an insecure channel. I am interested in studying stronger cryptographic primitives that may be guaranteed by no-cloning. An example is uncloneable encryption, which allows a classical message to be encoded in a quantum ciphertext in such a way that the no-cloning property prohibits the original classical message from being duplicated.
See for example arxiv:2503.19125, arxiv:2603.08916, arxiv:2111.08081
